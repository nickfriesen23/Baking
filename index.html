Weekend Baking Recipes

Croissants

For the Détrempe (dough):

  • 4⅔ cups/605 grams all-purpose or bread flour
  • ⅓ cup/66 grams granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon plus ½ teaspoon/12 grams kosher salt
  • 2¼ teaspoons/7 grams active dry yeast
  • ¾ cup plus 2 tablespoons/214 grams water, room temperature
  • ½ cup/120 grams whole milk, room temperature
  • ¼ cup/57 grams unsalted butter, cut into ½-inch pieces, chilled

For the Butter Block and Assembly:

  • 1½ cups/340 grams unsalted European butter (3 sticks), chilled
  • All-purpose flour, for rolling
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1 tablespoon heavy cream

Day 1:

  1. Mix flour, sugar, salt, yeast in stand mixer. Add water and milk. Mix 5 minutes until smooth.
  2. Add butter pieces, mix 8-10 minutes until very smooth and stretchy.
  3. Form dough ball, cut "+" on top, let rise 45min-1hr, then chill 4-12hrs.
  4. Make butter block: Shape butter into 8-inch square packet between parchment.

Day 2:

  1. Roll dough to 16x8 inches, place butter block in center.
  2. Enclose butter, roll to 24-inch long slab.
  3. Perform double turn, freeze 15min, refrigerate 1hr.
  4. Perform simple turn, freeze 15min, refrigerate 1hr.
  5. Roll to 14x17 inches, chill overnight.

Day 3:

  1. Cut dough into triangles.
  2. Shape croissants, proof 2-2½ hours at 70-75°F.
  3. Chill 20min, brush with egg wash.
  4. Bake at 375°F for 30-35 minutes until deeply browned.

Bagels

For the Dough:

  • 2¼ cups/530 milliliters lukewarm water (105 to 110 degrees)
  • 2 tablespoons barley malt syrup
  • 1 (¼-ounce) packet active dry yeast (about 2¼ teaspoons)
  • 6½ cups/885 grams bread flour (or use 6 cups bread flour and ½ cup whole-wheat flour)
  • 2 tablespoons/17 grams Diamond Crystal kosher salt or 1 tablespoon/17 grams Morton kosher salt
  • Neutral oil, for greasing

For Assembly:

  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ cup/60 milliliters barley malt syrup
  • Optional toppings: 2 ounces/30 grams each sesame seeds, poppy seeds, caraway seeds, dried minced garlic, dried minced onion and/or flaky salt
  1. Mix ½ cup lukewarm water with 2 tablespoons barley malt syrup and yeast. Let foam for 5 minutes.
  2. Combine flour and salt in large bowl. Add yeast mixture and remaining water (1¾ cups). Mix until shaggy.
  3. Knead 15-20 minutes until smooth and slightly tacky.
  4. Let rise until doubled, 1½-2 hours.
  5. Divide into 12 equal pieces (4⅓ oz each). Shape into balls.
  6. Form bagel rings by rolling into 9-inch ropes and connecting ends.
  7. Place on oiled parchment-lined sheets. Refrigerate 4-24 hours.
  8. Heat oven to 450°F. Bring water with baking soda and ¼ cup malt syrup to boil.
  9. Boil bagels 1 minute each, flipping halfway.
  10. Add toppings if desired.
  11. Bake 20-25 minutes until deeply brown, rotating halfway.

Biscuits

  • 230g or 1/2lb unsalted european butter (plugra or kerrygold) - frozen
  • 425g or 3.4c ap flour
  • 8g or 1 1/4tsp salt
  • 15g or 1 1/4Tbsp granulated sugar
  • 15g or 1Tbsp baking powder
  • 3g or 2/3tsp baking soda
  • 300g or 1 1/4c whole buttermilk
  1. Grate frozen butter on large holes of box grater.
  2. Add flour, salt, sugar, baking powder, and baking soda to a bowl and whisk together. Add frozen grated butter. Use your fingers to crumble butter flakes into the flour to create a gravely texture. Work quickly to keep from melting the butter.
  3. Drizzle in buttermilk while stirring the mixture.
  4. Flip the dough mix out onto a well floured work surface. Press the powdery mess of dough into a rough rectangle.
  5. Use a rolling pin to roll dough out into an 18"/45cm long, 6-7"/17cm wide rectangle.
  6. Fold the dough - fold top third down over the middle third, then fold bottom (unfolded dough) up over the now folded 2 layers.
  7. Rotate entire folded mass 90 degrees and use rolling pin to roll out into an 18"/45cm long rectangle again.
  8. Fold again as you did the first time. Rotate 90 degrees again and flip to the back side.
  9. Use rolling pin to roll into an 18"/45cm long rectangle a third time. Fold again.
  10. Rotate 90 degrees, roll, rotate 90 and roll. In total, you're rolling and folding 5 times.
  11. After the fifth fold, flip dough so the bottom side is facing up again and use the rolling pin to pound and flatten the dough just a bit.
  12. Place on a tray, cover with a towel, and refrigerate for about 10 minutes to firm up the butter and relax gluten.
  13. Place now cooled dough onto a floured worksurface and slowly roll out into a 10"x10" (25cm) square.
  14. Use a floured knife or bench scraper to cut dough into 9 even squares.
  15. Transfer squares to a parchment lined sheet tray. Preheat oven and freeze the cut biscuit dough for 20 minutes.
  16. After removing from freezer, brush tops of biscuit with melted butter then bake at 400F/205C for 20-24 minutes, rotating the tray halfway through baking.

Donuts

Dough:

  • 2 1/3 cups plus 2 tablespoons (295g) King Arthur Unbleached Cake Flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 3/4 teaspoon table salt, plus a pinch
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 large egg, at room temperature
  • 2 large egg yolks, at room temperature
  • 1/2 cup (99g) granulated sugar
  • 4 tablespoons (57g) unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 3/4 cup (170g) sour cream, at room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons King Arthur Pure Vanilla Extract

Chocolate Frosting:

  • 1 cup (113g) confectioners' sugar
  • 1/3 cup (28g) unsweetened cocoa
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1/3 cup (76g) sour cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Strawberry Frosting:

  • 1/2 cup (14g) freeze-dried strawberries
  • 1 1/2 cups (170g) confectioners' sugar
  • 1/4 cup (57g) sour cream
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • Pinch of salt

Vanilla Frosting:

  • 1/4 cup (57g) sour cream
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2 cups (227g) confectioners' sugar

For the Dough:

  1. Whisk together flour, baking powder, salt, baking soda, and nutmeg.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk egg, yolks, and sugar until light (2 minutes).
  3. Slowly stream in melted butter while whisking.
  4. Add dry ingredients and sour cream alternately in thirds.
  5. Transfer to piping bag.
  6. Pipe rings on parchment squares (3" across, 1" hole).
  7. Heat oil to 350°F.
  8. Fry 2 minutes per side until golden brown.

For the Frostings:

Chocolate:

Whisk confectioners' sugar, cocoa, and salt. Add sour cream and vanilla, whisk until smooth.

Strawberry:

Pulverize strawberries, sift. Mix with sugar, sour cream, lemon juice, and salt until smooth.

Vanilla:

Whisk sour cream, vanilla, lemon juice, and salt. Gradually add sugar until smooth.

Dip warm donuts in frosting, add sprinkles if desired. Best eaten same day.
